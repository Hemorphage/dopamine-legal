# Dopam_ne Privacy Policy

_Effective: June 13, 2026_

Dopam_ne ("the app") is a single-player reflex game published by Lucas Langlois.
This policy explains what information the app collects and how it's used.

## What we collect

- **An anonymous identifier.** On first launch, the app generates a random
  identifier (a UUID) and stores it securely in your device's Keychain. It is
  not linked to your name, email address, Apple ID, or any other personal
  information — there are no accounts and no sign-in.
- **Your scores.** When a run ends, the app sends that anonymous identifier and
  the run's score to our backend (hosted on Supabase) so the app can show your
  rank and a leaderboard of recent top scores.

We do not collect your name, email, location, contacts, photos, or any other
personal data. The app contains no advertising and no third-party analytics or
tracking SDKs.

## How this data is used

- To calculate your position on the rolling 24-hour leaderboard
- To display the top scores from the last 24 hours to all players

This data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or used to track you across
other apps or websites.

## Data retention

Scores are stored on our servers, associated only with your anonymous
identifier. They are used to compute the rolling 24-hour leaderboard; scores
older than 24 hours no longer affect anyone's rank.

## Your choices

Because there's no account, there's nothing on our end that's linked back to
you personally — your anonymous identifier is just a random number with no way
for us, or anyone else, to connect it to your identity. Uninstalling the app
discards the identifier stored in your device's Keychain; a fresh install
generates a new one.
